A little about me

Growing up, I took inspiration from everything I had available to me; reading books and encyclopedias occupied most of my time, and every once in a while a glimmer of wisdom would present itself in the most unexpected of places. One such glimmer of wisdom was a quote by Yuan-Cheng Fung, also known as the father of biomechanics, “Foundation of solid mechanics”,“A scientist will try to understand nature while an engineer stresses invention”. Empowered by this belief, my desire to contribute to society started to take a formal shape. My keenness towards machinery, design, and structure – thanks to my father, a mechanical engineer by profession – made me believe in my robust interests and convinced me to pursue a career in engineering. My academic journey at the University of Pennsylvania has equipped me with a solid foundation in the field, and I have had the opportunity to apply my knowledge through hands-on projects and research. In my quest for new challenges, I am driven to apply my knowledge and skills to address real-world issues, striving for advancements that foster the well-being and prosperity of humankind.

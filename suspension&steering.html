Suspension & Steering System of an All Wheel Drive All Terrain Vehicle

Team RedShift Racing India

Team RedShift Racing India is a group of automobile enthusiasts with a common goal of building an efficient single seater all terrain vehicle with an impeccable design, capable of competing at national and international level competitions.
I was a part of the team for 3 years during my undergraduate, joining as a suspension and steering engineer and quickly transitioning to the steering head of the team. As steering head, I was responsible for designing and fabricating a safe and efficient steering system for the ATV. I not only designed a unique steering system but also optimized the design and proved its structural reliability using Ansys (FEA Tool). Applying the theory of mechanical engineering taught to us in school, we found innovative solutions which helped us manufacture the first ever prototype of an All-Wheel-Drive ATV in the 10 year history of our team.

My contributions

Designing the Suspension and Steering System

I started the design process by considering the dynamic requirements from the suspension and steering systems, the packing inside the chassis with the drivertrain and braking components, suspension parameters including , trackwidth, roll centre, and DFMEA of previous designs. I made a 2D sketch to get the hardpoints, and analyzed these on Lotus Shark software to gauge their performance on bumps, droops and turns. After finalizing the hardpoints, I designed 3D models of individual components using SolidWorks.

Analyzing and Optimizing the Suspension and Steering System

The next step was to analyze the designed components to make sure they were structurally stable. For this, I used Ansys to perform static structural analysis on individual components, and transient analysis on suspension assemblies. Forces acting on the components were calculated using the rules and theories of vehicle dynamics. Suitable materials were chosen for all the components using the results from these analyses and Asbhy charts. The main goal here was to make a structurally safe design, and optimize it to be as light-weight as possible.

Manufacturing and Testing the Suspension and Steering System

After the finalizing the optimized designs and materials, detailed drawings were made which included the required tolerances. The components were manufactured using appropriate processes which including CNC milling, lathe machining, drilling, jig boring, gear cutting and anodizing (for aluminum parts). These components were then assembled on the chassis with other subsystems, and rigorous testing was performed.

Leadership and Training

As a part of the core leadership team, I trained a group of 25 junior team members to safely operate tools and machinery. I was also responsible for training a team of 5 juniors who would take over the suspension and steering subsystem after I graduate.

Inventory Management

Being a student team, we had a lot of financial restrictions. Due to this we had to make sure we use our limited resources, which included time, efforts and capital wisely. Having an efficient inventory management system was a must to achieve this goal. I took up the role of being the inventory manager, and used the 5S technique of inventory management taught in one of my classes to sort, segregate, shine, standardize and sustain the resources present with us in our workshop. This led to a significantly cleaner, well organised workshop; and made it a lot easier to locate tools and fasteners saving time and effort.
